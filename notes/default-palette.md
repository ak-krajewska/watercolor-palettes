# Default Palette — Notes & Observations

## Primary Use Cases

- **Plein air landscapes** — San Francisco locations
- **Urban sketching**
- **Loose botanicals** — usually painted alongside the landscape sessions: typically 3 sheets at once to resist overworking, with 2 landscape views and 1 whatever-is-at-my-feet plants

This palette is optimized for working outdoors in SF, often doing multiple subjects in one session.

### Priority hierarchy

Plein air landscapes carry the highest penalty for a wrong palette decision — if the hike to Mt Davidson reveals a missing color, that's a real loss. Urban sketching and cafe work are forgiving (a mediocre croissant sketch is fine). Botanicals painted at home are lowest stakes: the full collection is a few steps away.

This means the default palette is correctly optimized for plein air first. The botanicals are underserved by it, but that's acceptable — a dedicated botanical palette is the right fix, not changing the defaults.

## Indispensable

**Permanent Rose** (W&N, PV19) — can't live without it. One of the workhorses. Also the base for my go-to orange: Permanent Rose + Cadmium Yellow.

**Cadmium Yellow** (W&N) — can't live without it. Mixes the orange above; also essential with Sap Green for yellow-leaning greens.

**Yellow Ochre** (W&N) — can't live without it.

**Gouache / Primary White** (Holbein) — almost always ends up in use, especially for still lives.

**Indigo** (CfM) — by far the most important convenience mix on the palette. Functions as an effective black and general darkener. Took over the role Payne's Gray used to fill — actually replaced Payne's Gray on this palette for exactly that reason.

## Blues

Three blues on the palette: Ultramarine, Cobalt Sea Blue, Cerulean.

Since adding the **Cobalt Sea Blue** (Aquarius), **Cerulean** (CfM) barely gets used. It's a color I like, but I never seem to mix with it — it just sits there. Probably a candidate for removal on a future edit, or worth experimenting with intentionally.

## Greens

**Sap Green** (W&N) — butt ugly straight from the pan, but a reliable base for mixing good greens. Direction of the mix determines the partner: Cobalt Sea Blue for blue-leaning greens, Cadmium Yellow for yellow-leaning greens.

**CfM Olive** used to live on this palette. Cobalt Sea Blue bumped it. Doesn't need a slot because Sap Green + Indigo reproduces it well enough — and Olive is a great color in its own right, just not palette-essential.

## The Orange Workflow

Orange isn't on the palette as a tube color — it gets mixed: **Permanent Rose + Cadmium Yellow**. This comes up a lot, particularly for the orange mugs I paint at the cafe.

**Dioxazine Violet** (W&N) is the shading color for that orange. Could theoretically live without it, but don't want to. Also earns its place for flowers — useful when painting landscapes with floral elements. Worth noting it's dangerously staining — handle with intent.

## Underperformers

**Cadmium Red** (W&N) — used far less than expected. Not a cadmium-aversion thing (Cad Yellow and Cobalt Blue get used constantly). Notably doesn't mix well — Cad Red + Cad Yellow produces a dull orange. By itself though it's brilliant, thins well, and mixes well with white, so it has a place in more opaque/gouache-heavy work. Just not a go-to mixer.

**Alizarin Crimson** (W&N) — barely used. Kept on the palette on principle (should have a cool red) but in practice it just doesn't get reached for. A candidate for honest removal.

**Burnt Sienna** (W&N) — theoretically loved, actually underused. Does come out for coffee and landscapes, but it's a bit staining so it ends up getting bypassed in favor of Yellow Ochre. The staining makes it feel less safe to throw around loosely.

**Cyprus Burnt Umber** (Aquarius) — a nice color, but also used less than expected.

## Colors That Don't Mix Well

**Cerulean** (CfM) — beautiful for sky, but doesn't mix well. Consistent with its low use as a mixer generally. Best used relatively straight.

**Cadmium Red** (W&N) — see above. The Cad Red + Cad Yellow orange is notably dull compared to the Permanent Rose + Cad Yellow orange.

## Open Questions

**Sap Green** — could theoretically do without it. Actively considering whether a different convenience green would serve better.
