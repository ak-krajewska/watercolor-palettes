# CfM Floral Palette — Notes

## Overview

A floral palette built around CfM Handmade Watercolors, originally based on CfM's own proposed palette with slight modifications. Lived in the Yellow (CfM Simple Travel Palette, 12 slots).

**Status: retired / reference.** Didn't perform as well as hoped. Useful as a starting point for designing a better botanical palette.

## The Paints

Poppy, French Vermilion, Coral, Fuchsia, Lavender Blue Lake, Lupine, Ultramarine Pink, Hot Pink, Oxalis, Meadow, Tide Pool, Cypress Green — all CfM.

## What Didn't Work

**French Vermilion isn't a good substitute for Cadmium Red.** CfM avoids cadmium colors, and the palette reflects that — but the Vermilion (PR4+PY1) just doesn't perform the same way. Cad Red has a brilliance and thinning quality that French Vermilion doesn't match.

**The yellow (Oxalis) is OK but not great.** Same constraint: no cadmium, so no Cad Yellow. The CfM Oxalis is fine but again, not the same.

## What Was Good

The color selection is beautiful in theory — lots of pinks, violets, and soft greens that are genuinely lovely for florals. The CfM palette is pretty. It just didn't hold up as a working palette.

## Lessons for the Next Botanical Palette

- Probably needs Cad Yellow and Permanent Rose from W&N as anchors
- French Vermilion is not a drop-in for Cad Red — if a red-orange is needed, use the real thing
- The CfM colors that are genuinely useful (Lupine, Ultramarine Pink, the pinks) could still appear alongside stronger W&N paints
